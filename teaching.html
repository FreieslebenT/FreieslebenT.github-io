Teaching

I really enjoy teaching because it's the best way to lose the narrow and singular mindset that you sometimes have in research. Instead of focusing alone on a small subtopic, teaching is about putting ideas into context and discussing them interactively with smart students. Also, teaching forces you to explain things in simple terms, which in Feynman's sense is the only way to really understand them. In recent years I have taught several courses, both for philosophy and statistics students:

  • Explainable Artificial Intelligence, winter term 21/22, philosophy and statistics department, syllabus.
  • Causality and Machine Learning, summer term 21, statistics department, syllabus.
  • Philosophy of Artificial Intelligence, winter term 20/21, philosophy department, syllabus.
  • Ethics of Artificial Intelligence, summer term 20, statistics department, syllabus.

I also helped design the lecture 'Interpretable Machine Learning' at the LMU statistics department. Previously, I have also assisted in various different courses, both for philosophy and mathematics students:

  • Models and Simulations, summer term 20, philosophy department, syllabus.
  • Central Topics in Philosophy of Science (CTPS), philosophy department, syllabus.
  • Linear Algebra I, II and Analysis I, II, 2016-2019, mathematics department.

In the courses I have taught, I have created some additional materials, such as a small introductory Youtube course on Netlogo , a formal script. for the CTPS course, and a simple example for students who want to get started with Latex.
