Timo Freiesleben


Hi visitor, this is me, Timo Freiesleben and I welcome you on my personal website. I am a postdoc in the philosophy and ethics group at the Machine Learning in Science Cluster at the University of Tübingen with Dr. Thomas Grote.

I am interested in topics like:

  • Interpretable Machine Learning or Explainable AI
  • Philosophy of Science and Causality
  • Ethics of AI and Algorithmic Fairness
  • Philosophy and Theory of Machine Learning

I am also an external member of the Munich Center for Mathematical Philosophy (LMU Munich), where I did my PhD under the supervision of Prof. Stephan Hartmann on the question what explainable AI explains.

I studied math, philosophy of science, computer science and some neuroscience in Tübingen, Oslo, and Munich. You can find my complete CV here (Version 08-2024). Beyond machine learning and philosophy of science, I am interested in classical literature, modern physics, existentialist philosophy, football, stand-up-paddling, snorkerling, Vietnamese food, cooking, board games, chess, Rubicks cube, and many more things.
